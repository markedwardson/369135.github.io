My Design Philosophy

I enjoy designing spaces that offer a sense of order, balance, and quiet beauty.

No matter the project size and budget, I focus on the design elements that give a garden a sense of timelessness combined with a modern sensibility.

  • An orderly frame that is constant, while the content is dynamic. If the bones of the garden are well placed, the garden has year-round interest and plantings have a stronger presence in the blooming season. I use hedging and crisp lines to create a strong frame for more naturalistic plantings.

  • Classic aesthetic principles, such as strong axial lines, focal points, framed views, juxtaposition of light and dark, and a sense of geometry and balance achieved through symmetry or asymmetry.

  • Site-appropriate lush plantings that soften the structural elements. While a restrained color palette conveys a sense of unity and cohesion in the garden, a variety of textures and shapes can add interest and movement.
